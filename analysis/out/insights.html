Fingerprints
in the data

The leaderboard ranks players by era-adjusted major wins. But the ratios between those numbers — rings to wins, peak to career, raw rank to adjusted rank — carry a second layer of information. Seeded by one observation (JKap wins a lopsided share of World Championships relative to his overall résumé), here are the other signatures hiding in the same 156 majors.

Built from site/data.js (reconciled per-player season breakdowns) joined to major_events.json for LAN/region metadata, via analysis/insights.py. Ratio boards apply minimum-volume filters (noted per card) so a single-win player can't top a rate stat. Runner-up and finals records aren't shown — team_participation.json carries team placements but no player rosters, so second-place finishes can't be traced to individuals.
